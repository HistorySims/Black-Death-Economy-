HOW IT WORKS: IN TOWN (RENTS)

Goal

Finish the run with the most gold. Your one lever is rent. Charge too much and your building goes empty. Charge too little and you “win” tenants but leave money on the table.

This is a bargaining-power story: before the plague, tenants have more options; after the plague, landlords and survivors renegotiate everything.

Controls (what to click)

  • Use - and + to set your rent.
  • Your building is outlined orange.
  • The top bar shows Date, Population, My Wealth, and Avg Rent.

If “How it Works” ever takes you into a running game, come back here—this launcher is the instructions hub.

How the simulation behaves

  • Each month, tenants shuffle between houses looking for better deals.
  • When population drops (the plague), there are fewer renters overall, so demand falls.
  • Rival landlords also change rent, so you’re in a price war.

Watch the relationship between Population and Avg Rent. That’s the “market pressure” indicator.

Simple strategy experiments (student-friendly)

  • High early rent: push rent above average and see when emptiness begins.
  • Match the market: hover near Avg Rent and try to avoid vacancy.
  • Plague shock response: when population collapses, drop rent and see if occupancy returns.
Ask: “Who has leverage right now?”
Ask: “What changed after 1348?”

What to write down (worksheet-ready)

  • Before 1348: what rent level kept you full?
  • After 1348: what rent level kept you full?
  • Did your “best” move feel unfair or morally gross? Why might real landlords do it anyway?
  • How did the plague change bargaining power?

HOW IT WORKS: IN THE FIELDS (WAGES)

Goal

Harvest grapes and end with the most gold. Your one lever is wage offered. Pay too low and you can’t staff your rows. Pay too high and you “win” workers but burn your profits.

The plague flips leverage here too—after mass death, workers become scarce, so wages rise.

Controls (what to click)

  • Use - and + to set your offered wage.
  • Top bar shows Year, Workers, Gold, and Avg Wage.
  • Other vineyards post their pay above the hill line.

How the simulation behaves

  • Workers drift to the best-paying vineyard (“poaching”).
  • You need enough workers to harvest effectively.
  • When the Black Death hits, the total worker pool shrinks fast.

If you suddenly can’t fill jobs even at high pay, that’s the point: scarcity can make “money” less useful than “people.”

Simple strategy experiments

  • Low-wage gamble: stay under Avg Wage and see how much you lose to poaching.
  • Match the market: keep wage near Avg Wage and watch stability.
  • Plague shock response: increase wage sharply after the plague and see if staffing returns.
Ask: “Why do wages spike?”
Ask: “Who can say ‘no’ now?”

What to write down (worksheet-ready)

  • What wage kept you fully staffed before the plague?
  • What wage kept you staffed after the plague?
  • Did you ever raise wages and still fail to hire? What does that tell you about supply limits?
  • How did the plague change the power relationship between employers and workers?
